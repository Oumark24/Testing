The Heart Behind It

A grandfather's love became our mission

"He believed we didn't love him anymore. That broke me — and it changed everything."

KindNest was born from a deeply personal experience. My grandfather — a man I love deeply — has schizophrenia, a condition that shapes the way he thinks, feels, and connects with the world around him.

During that time, he came to believe that our family had stopped loving him. That pain — real to him, heartbreaking to witness — stayed with me. It made me ask: how many other people in nursing homes and care facilities feel exactly this way, right now?

The answer was clear. Loneliness and isolation among the elderly is a quiet crisis. Many residents go days, weeks, or longer without a meaningful visit or a genuine moment of human warmth. Mental health challenges can intensify that sense of abandonment.

KindNest exists to change that — one visit, one care basket, one conversation at a time. We believe that presence is powerful. Showing up — sitting beside someone, looking them in the eye, asking how they're doing — is a profound act of kindness that costs nothing but means everything.

What We Do

Three pillars of kindness

KindNest's work is built around three simple but powerful commitments to those who need it most.

🏡

Personal Visits

We show up in person. Regular, consistent visits to nursing home residents — not just a wave from the door, but real time spent together. Listening. Talking. Being present.

🎁

Care Baskets

Thoughtfully assembled packages filled with items residents actually need and appreciate. Small things that say: someone thought of you. Someone remembered you.

🌱

Community of Care

Building a network of volunteers, donors, and nursing home partners who share the belief that no person — regardless of age or health — should feel alone and forgotten.

The Road Ahead

How we're building KindNest

1

Lay the Foundation

Research how to formally establish KindNest as an organization — building a website, understanding legal requirements, and creating a framework for donations and support.

2

Connect with Nursing Homes

Reach out to local care facilities — starting with the one where my grandfather stays — to establish visit schedules and understand resident needs firsthand.

3

Deliver Care Packages

Begin assembling and bringing care baskets to residents. Each package is personal, practical, and filled with the message: you are not forgotten.

4

Grow the Community

Expand our network of volunteers and donors, scaling the impact of KindNest across more nursing homes and reaching more people who need connection.
